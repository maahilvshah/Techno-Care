Malaria

Malaria is caused by a single-celled parasite of the genus plasmodium. The parasite is transmitted to humans most commonly through mosquito bites.

Symptoms of Malaria

Pain areas: in the abdomen or muscles
Whole body: chills, fatigue, fever, night sweats, shivering, or sweating
Gastrointestinal: diarrhoea, nausea, or vomiting
Also common: fast heart rate, headache, mental confusion.

Specialist Doctor

1) Infectious Disease Doctor : Treats infections, including those that are in tropical nature.
2) Primary Care Provider : Prevents,Diagnosis and treats diseases.
3) Emergency Medicine Doctor : Treats patients in the emergency department.

Medicine

The most common antimalarial drugs include: Chloroquine phosphate. Chloroquine is the preferred treatment for any parasite that is sensitive to the medicine.

There are some other medicines also, take them on doctors prescription only
